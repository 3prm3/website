How to make a VHS effect...

What you'll need:

Paint.net

For this tutorial, I'm gonna be using an image of a deer.

Photo by Laura College on Unsplash

First: Open your image

Next: CTRL+C your image

After this, go to Layers and click the square with the plus sign in the corner.

At this point, you should have two layers with your image

For your first image (one on the lower layer) you should press CTRL+SHIFT+G on it, this should make the image Black and White.

After this, press CTRL+SHIFT+T on your image, this should bring up the Brightness/Contrast menu.

Here, you should click the text bok and set the Brightness to 50 and click ok

From here, we can go to editing the upper layer...

First, you should press CTRL+C on your upper layer

After this, you should make a new file by pressing CTRL+N

Doing this should bring up a "New" prompt, press OK at the bottom of the prompt and you should see a blank canvas, at this blank canvas, you should press CTRL+V and your image should appear

At your image, you should press CTRL+SHIFT+U

Doing this shold bring up this menu, here, you should open the text box and set the Saturation to 200 and then press OK again.

After this, you should bring up the Brightness prompt again with CTRL+SHIFT+T and set the Brightness to 200.

When you're done setting the Brightness, you should divide your image width by six and remember that number.

Now, press CTRL+R, uncheck maintain aspect ration and set the image width to itself divided by six.

At this point you should go back to your two-layered image and press CTRL+X on it to remove the original image from the layer.

Now, go back to the squished image and press CTRL+C on it, go back to the original (two-layered) image again and press CTRL+V on the second layer.

Press CTRL+A on your image and set the Quality near the top corner of the screen to Bilinear. Now you should extend your image until it fits the canvas and completely covers the Black and White image

Now, you shoud go to Layers again and click on the wrench

After clicking on the wrench, this menu should show up.

Here, you should click on the word "Normal" and scroll down until you find the word Multiply. Click on the word Multiply and press OK.

Go back to the Layers section and click on the two pieces of a box with an arrow in.

This will merge your two layers into one.

Now, press CTRL+SHIFT+S, name your file and press "Save"
